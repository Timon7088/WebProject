(Xbox Series X and the Xbox Series S) The home video game consoles developed by Microsoft, released on November 10, 2020, as the fourth generation of the Xbox console family, succeeding the Xbox One. The Xbox Series X is powered by a custom 7 nm AMD Zen 2 CPU with eight cores running at a nominal 3.8 GHz or, when simultaneous multithreading (SMT) is used, at 3.6 GHz. One CPU core is dedicated to the underlying operating system. The Xbox Series X's console form is designed to be unobtrusive and minimalistic.
